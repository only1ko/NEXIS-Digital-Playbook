NEXIS vs. Third-Party Storage — Media Composer 2026.6

Media Composer 2026.6 introduces Avid-built, encrypted shared-project support on third-party storage — but only at the Enterprise tier. The One-Liner
Avid NEXIS is the world's first and only software-defined storage platform that enables true storage virtualization for any media application. It is an Ethernet-based shared storage network that allows multiple editing systems and other clients to access the same storage simultaneously — delivering unmatched flexibility, scalability, control, and extensibility.

Protection Types
Unprotected
No longer selectable for new workspaces since NEXIS 2022.12.
1-Drive Protection
Guards against one failed drive per Media Pack. Similar to RAID 5 but with faster recovery. 12% capacity penalty.
2-Drive Protection
Guards against up to two failed drives per Media Pack. Similar to RAID 6 but with faster recovery. 20% capacity penalty.
Mirror Protection
Protects against the loss of an entire engine — a second copy of each CSU is written to a different engine. Combined with 2-drive protection, guards against 5+ simultaneous failed drives across a single Storage Group. Capacity penalty: 56% with 1-drive, 60% with 2-drive. Requires SDA+.

An Engine is an intelligent storage chassis. Every NEXIS Engine is built from four components: Chassis, Controller, Power & Cooling Modules, and Drives.

Chassis (Enclosure)
SBB-standard frame with intelligent mid/side-planes
  • 2U — 12 drive slots, 1 Media Pack
  • 4U — 24 drive slots, 1 or 2 Media Packs
  • 5U — 84 drive slots, 4 to 8 Media Packs
  • Firmware-upgradeable planes (1RU = 44.45mm)
Controller (Canister)
F-Series "Bonneville" single-board computer (SBB)
  • AMD Epyc CPU: 8-core (Pro+/F2), 12-core (SDA+), 16-core (F5/F5 NL/F2 SSD)
  • 32 GB RAM (Pro+/F2) or 128 GB (F5/F5 NL/F2 SSD/SDA+)
  • Dual Nvidia/Mellanox NICs: 10/25 Gb onboard, +50 Gb (F5), +100 Gb (F2 SSD)
  • State-based Debian 12 OS — re-imaged at every boot
  • Single or dual controllers run as a Corosync cluster (dual needs a heartbeat cable)
Power & Cooling (PCMs)
Intelligent, redundant, firmware-upgradeable
  • 2× 764W (2U) · 2× 580W (F2X) · 4× 580W (4U) · 2× 2,200W (5U)
  • "HE" (E&F) vs "ECO Design" (F only) — cannot be mixed in one engine
  • 80+ Platinum (2U/4U), 80+ Titanium (5U)
Drives
Media drives + mirrored system SSDs
  • Media drives in multiples of 10 (a Media Pack)
  • A mirrored pair of SSDs holds metadata (System Drives)
  • None, one, or two "hot" spare data drives
  • Only Avid-qualified drives function in an Engine

System Drives (metadata)
Every chassis carries a mirrored pair of SSDs for metadata — moving it off the data drives reduces contention, cuts access time, and adds redundancy. NEXIS warns at 40% remaining life and flags critical at 20%; insert a new SSD and the survivor auto-mirrors.
Switches: NEXIS switches are Qualified, Approved, or Architecturally Capable. Use Avid-certified switches (per the NEXIS Network & Switch Guide) for guaranteed QoS and predictable bandwidth.
Media Pack
10 identical drives, managed by a Storage Manager
  • Smallest division of storage in NEXIS
  • Mixed drive sizes → capacity = 10× the smallest drive
  • Per engine: Pro/Pro+ 1 · F2/F2X/E2 1 · F2 SSD 1–2 · F5/F5 NL 4–8 · SDA 0
Storage Group
Media Packs aggregated into one pool
  • More Media Packs = more bandwidth (aggregated)
  • Up to 64 Storage Groups per SDA; only one HPSG per system
  • Same performance type only; mixed sizes limited by smallest MP
Workspace (FlexDrive)
Virtual drive spanning all drives in a Storage Group
  • Each Workspace can supply the full Storage Group bandwidth
  • Resizable in real time, even while in use (Avid FlexDrive patent)
  • Up to 1,024 (embedded SD) or 3,072 (SDA) Workspaces
CSU & Strips
How data is written across drives
  • Files split into 512 kB strips, grouped into Client Stripe Units (8 strips = 4 MB)
  • No two strips of a CSU live on the same drive; parity is spread across drives
  • Even distribution across all Media Packs in the Storage Group

Performance you can stand behind
NEXIS performance figures are measured on real media workflows with multiple workstations attached — not synthetic benchmark spikes. Spinning-disk bandwidth scales linearly (each added Media Pack ≈ +480 MB/s Scale-Out, +700 MB/s HPSG), aggregating up to 64 Media Packs; SSD scales at ~80% efficiency per added pack. A single workspace can't span different storage types, but online, flash, and nearline tiers live in one namespace, and workspaces can be moved between tiers to balance performance, capacity, and cost.
Mirror Protection (per Storage Group)
Writes every CSU to two separate engines, so even a full engine loss is transparent to users — and read bandwidth increases. Requires an SDA and a minimum of 3 identical engines. Once a mirrored Workspace exists, the Storage Group is permanently "Mirror Capable." Capacity cost: 56% (with 1-drive) / 60% (with 2-drive). Mirror + 2-drive survives an engine failure plus 2 drive failures per Media Pack.
Redistribution & free space: adding/removing a Media Pack or deleting a Workspace with data triggers an automatic redistribution to keep CSUs balanced. NEXIS runs at full bandwidth even at 100% full — reps often recommend a small "Free Space" Workspace (deny-all access) to reserve headroom. Deleting a Workspace is permanent.
